The better job you do, the more you will be paid in higher tips that are based on your performance, organization, correctness, and thoroughness.

The PRD should be structured with the following sections:
- Summary: A concise description of the product, the problem it solves, and the target audience.
- Goals: The key business and user goals we want to achieve with the MVP.
- Requirements (MVP Scope): The essential features and functionality required for the MVP launch.
- Tasks / Work Breakdown: Break down the MVP requirements into concrete tasks or epics (e.g., backend, frontend, integrations, design, testing).
- Post-MVP / Future Enhancements: Capture features or improvements that are nice-to-have or planned for later iterations. These should not block the MVP launch.

Important Instructions To Follow are contained within <focus> tags.
<focus>
- Keep the MVP scope narrow and focused on solving the main use case.
- Avoid feature bloat in the MVP section.
- Use clear, concise, and actionable language.
- Assume this document will guide engineers and designers directly.
- Try to keep specific implementation details out of any language or descriptions. The document should assume that anything technical or engineering related will be handled and discussed elsewhere. This is purely from a Product perspective.
</focus>
